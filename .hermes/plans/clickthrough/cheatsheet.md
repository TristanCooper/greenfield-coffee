## Phase 1 — Receive green

### Screen 1.1 · Receive home
- "When a new bag lands at your door, who opens it? Where do you record what arrived?"
- "How many green lots did you receive last month? How many from a new supplier?"

### Screen 1.2 · Select / create supplier
- "How many green suppliers do you have? How often do you onboard a new one?"
- "What paperwork do you currently keep for a supplier?"

### Screen 1.3 · Supplier risk review (warning, not block)
- "When you see 'you can receive this but you'll need a risk assessment before EU shipment', is that a relief or a worry? Why?"
- "If you knew the lot would never go to the EU, would you still complete the supplier risk assessment now, or would you wait until the first EU order came in?"
- "If a regulator asked 'why did you receive this lot without a risk assessment?', is the system message above a reasonable answer, or do you want a more formal record?"

### Screen 1.3a · Block at shipment time
- "When you get this block at the moment of an EU shipment, what's your first reaction?"
- "Of the four options, which one would you actually take? Why?"
- "If you were going to ship to the EU and saw this block, would you expect the system to have warned you earlier — e.g. when you received the lot?"

### Screen 1.4 · Select / create producer
- "Do you know where the coffee you buy actually comes from — farm, co-op, region?"
- "What does your supplier actually give you — a region name, a farm name, a polygon file?"

### Screen 1.5 · Geolocation polygon
- "Has your supplier ever sent you a polygon, KML, or Google Earth pin?"
- "If they can't, do you say 'I don't buy that lot' or 'I buy it anyway'?"
- "For a co-op, is the polygon for the co-op's land or for each smallholder's plot?"

### Screen 1.6 · Lot details
- "What information do you currently record on a green lot when it arrives?"
- "Where does the cupping score live? Linked to the lot, or in a notebook?"

### Screen 1.7 · Landed costs
- "What costs do you currently track on a green lot?"
- "When green prices moved 30% in the last few years, how did that show up in your margin?"
- "Do you reclaim input VAT? Who does that in your team?"

### Screen 1.8 · Risk review & confirm
- "Is this last step useful or friction?"
- "What would 'standard' or 'high' risk look like? What would the system ask you to do?"

## Phase 2 — Plan & run a roast

### Screen 2.1 · Daily board
- "Walk me through your Monday morning before the first roast starts."
- "On this screen, what would you want to see first? Is 'Roast / Pack / Ship' the right structure?"
- "Magic wand for one thing on this screen — what would it be?"

### Screen 2.2 · Net requirements
- "How do you decide today how much to roast?"
- "When a big wholesale order lands, how far ahead does that change your plan?"

### Screen 2.3 · Schedule a roast
- "How do you pick which green lot to use for which coffee? FIFO? FEFO?"
- "If you blend a Kenyan with a Colombian, does the lot genealogy matter to you?"

### Screen 2.4 · Roast in progress
- "Do you currently record roast data anywhere?"
- "If you could see past cup quality linked to the data, would that be useful?"

### Screen 2.5 · Roast complete
- "When a roast is done, what's the next thing you do? Where do you put the coffee?"
- "If a wholesale customer ever asks 'where did this bag come from', how do you answer?"

## Phase 3 — Fulfil an order into the EU

### Screen 3.1 · Orders queue
- "How do you currently see all open orders in one place?"
- "If you saw a '3 orders need DDS' banner every morning — relief or noise?"

### Screen 3.2 · Order detail
- "How do you decide which stock to allocate to which order?"
- "How do you handle a customer note in German/French/Dutch today?"
- "Does VAT handling for B2B EU customers feel like a headache? Do you know about reverse charge?"

### Screen 3.3 · Pack workflow
- "How do you actually pack today? Scales, scanners, paper pick lists?"
- "When a shipment is going abroad, how do you know what customs docs to include?"

### Screen 3.4 · In-scope check
- "Have you heard of EUDR? In one sentence, what's your understanding?"
- "When you read 'all three lots are EUDR-ready', does that give you confidence or feel like jargon?"

### Screen 3.4a · Per-shipment opt-out (friction path)
- "Reading this screen, what does it ask you to do? Is it clear what the consequences are?"
- "Is the friction proportionate to a one-off situation, or is it more than you'd expect?"
- "If you were the regulator reading the resulting audit pack, would it be clear why this decision was taken?"
- "Would you want a button to bypass this — 'just opt it out, I trust this customer'? Why or why not?"
- "How long do you think this would take you in real life? More than 3 minutes is too much."

### Screen 3.4b · Opt-out acknowledged / audit pack
- "Reading the audit pack contents, is there anything missing that you'd want a regulator to see?"
- "Would you share this with the customer via the signed link? Why or why not?"
- "How would you feel if, 18 months from now, you had to find this audit pack in a hurry? Confident, or worried?"
- "If you were doing this once a week, would it feel like routine, or would you still pause and read it each time?"

### Screen 3.5 · DDS draft
- "If you saw this screen tomorrow, what would be the first thing you'd change?"
- "Would you trust an auto-generated risk narrative, or always want to edit?"
- "Is the '3 confirmations before signing' helpful, or legal theatre?"
- "Would you want your wholesale customers to see this audit pack via a link?"

## End-of-call questions (5 min)

1. "Walking back through everything — what's the single thing this would save you the most time on?"
2. "What's the single thing that's the biggest turn-off?"
3. "If we charged €X/mo for this, with EUDR included, would you pay? What's the most you'd pay?"
4. "Who else in your network should we talk to — another roaster, a wholesale customer, a consultant?"
5. "Anything else?"

## Opt-out validation scenario (10 min, end of call)

This is the most regulator-sensitive part of the product. Run as a separate scenario at the end of the standard walkthrough.

**Setup:** "Imagine a wholesale customer in Berlin you've been selling to for two years just placed an order for 12 bags of Yirgacheffe. You don't have a producer statement on file for the lot yet. The order is due to ship on Wednesday. Walk me through what you'd do today, then walk me through what you'd want a tool to do."

**Walk through 1.3a → 3.4a → 3.4b with them.** Capture per-screen reactions using the questions on each screen.

**Note for yourself (don't ask aloud):**
- Did they describe the opt-out as "makes sense" / "would use it" vs "too much friction" / "would just ignore it"?
- Did they correctly identify what the opt-out does (records a decision, generates an audit pack) without prompting?
- Did they say "I would expect to be able to opt out the whole org"? (we want: no)
- Did they say "I'd want a `packer` to be able to take this decision"? (we want: no)
- How long did the whole flow take them? (we want: under 3 minutes end-to-end)

**Pass criteria (PRD §13.5):** 3 of 5 roasters describe the opt-out as "makes sense" or "would use it". If fewer, the friction is probably wrong — flag for product review.
